In danger of extinction

The two species of African elephants, the forest elephant and the savannah elephant, have experienced a worrying decline in their populations in recent decades due to various reasons, mainly related to human activity.

Some of the main threats to African elephants include:

Poaching: Illegal hunting of elephants for their ivory tusks is one of the biggest threats. Ivory is highly valued in some markets, creating an economic incentive for poaching.

Habitat loss: Human expansion, agriculture and deforestation have led to habitat loss for elephants, limiting their range and access to food resources.

In danger of extinction

The vaquita porpoise is one of the most endangered species in the world, and its critical situation has led to numerous research and conservation efforts.

Various organizations, governments and scientists are working together to study the biology, eco logy and behavior of the vaquita, as well as to implement conservation measures to protect it.

It is essential that research on the vaquita porpoise continues to better understand its needs and develop effective conservation strategies. International collaboration and the enforcement of stricter laws and regulations are essential to address the imminent threat of extinction facing this unique species.
